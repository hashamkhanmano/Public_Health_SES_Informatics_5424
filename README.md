# Public_Health_SES_Informatics_5424
Population & Public Health Research: Impact of SES on Health Informatics
Project Overview
This project explores the critical relationship between Socioeconomic Status (SES) and the effectiveness of Health Informatics in modern healthcare. As digital health interventions become standard, this research examines whether these technologies are bridging the healthcare gap or inadvertently widening the "digital divide."

Research Objectives
Analyze how SES influences health outcomes and access to medical technology.

Evaluate the cost-effectiveness of digital health interventions (e.g., Telehealth, SMS reminders, and EHRs).

Identify systemic challenges such as algorithmic bias and the 29% rural gap in AI access.

Propose future research opportunities in Social Determinants of Health (SDOH) integration and value-based care models.

Key Findings & Methodology
Methodology: A comprehensive review of current public health literature and data from organizations such as the CDC, WHO, and JAMIA.

Cost Impact: Research indicates that 82% of digital health interventions are cost-effective, primarily by shifting the focus toward preventative care.

Quality Impact: Health informatics improves care coordination and reduces medication errors, though effectiveness varies based on a patient's technical literacy and access.

The "Digital Divide": A significant barrier remains, as nearly one-third of rural adults lack access to advanced AI-driven healthcare tools.

Technologies & Concepts Discussed
Electronic Health Records (EHRs): For improved clinical decision-making.

FHIR (Fast Healthcare Interoperability Resources): For real-time data sharing.

AI & Machine Learning: Potential for improved diagnostics vs. the risk of data bias.

Telehealth: A tool for reaching underserved populations.
